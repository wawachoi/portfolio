Wechat Mini Program - Happy Ripple

A WeChat-style assistant that helps socially anxious users craft the right message — in the right tone — for the right person.

The Problem

What does social anxiety look like in the digital age?

Texting should be simple — but for many, it isn't. Socially anxious users often overthink, hesitate, or avoid texting altogether.

"I rewrote this message five times."

"I don't want to sound rude."

"Can someone just tell me what to say?"

Such everyday anxieties show why a tool like HAPPY RIPPLE is needed — to help socially anxious users reply when words don't come easily.

User Insights

Users don't just struggle with "what to say," they struggle with how to say it. → A message to a boss needs to be formal and respectful, while a reply to a friend can be casual and warm.

Socially anxious users fear sounding "wrong" or "inappropriate." → Clear tone guidance to reduce hesitation and build confidence. → Low-effort, instant generation to ease overthinking.

One-size-fits-all responses don't work. → Context-aware replies that adapt to different relationships (boss, friend, family) and tone (formal / casual / playful).

Generic AI replies often feel too long, too stiff, or "not me." → Editable, customizable outputs that still "sound like me"

Structure & Flow

Minimize decisions and maximize confidence

→ Step 1: Open app→ Select who you're talking to (e.g., boss, friend)

→ (Optional) Adjust or edit the reply tone (formal, casual, playful, or custom)

→ Step 2: Type/paste what the other person said

→ Step 3: Get a full, polished message — ready to send

Brand Value

Brand Color

Primary
Secondary
Primary
Purple #8F33FF

Compared to blue, purple retains the rationality and professionalism of cool tones while adding warmth and creative expression, aligning with the identity of an AI-assisted communication tool. Neuroscience research (MIT) shows that blue–purple tones can activate both the prefrontal cortex (rational decision-making) and the limbic system (emotional resonance), boosting decision confidence by 27% for people with social anxiety. Thus, purple effectively conveys safety and low psychological pressure.

Secondary
Yellow #FFCC00

Acts as the accent color to emphasize warmth and sociability, visually adding vitality and excitement while enhancing shareability and memorability. Its high contrast with purple ensures clarity even in dark mode and meets the needs of high recognition and viral spread in social applications, turning color itself into a free communication channel.
